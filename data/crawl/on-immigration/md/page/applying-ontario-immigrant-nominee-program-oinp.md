# Applying to the Ontario Immigrant Nominee Program ( OINP)

Learn how to submit an application for a nomination for permanent residence in Ontario.

## On this page Skip this page navigation

---

## Regulatory amendments coming into force May 30

Please note that amendments to the regulations under the Ontario Immigration Act take effect May 30. These updates prepare for the redesign of the Ontario Immigrant Nominee Program (OINP). Please stay tuned to the [program updates page](https://www.ontario.ca/page/2026-ontario-immigrant-nominee-program-updates) for any further announcements.

## Overview

The Ontario Immigrant Nominee program uses two intake systems to manage applications. Before you can apply to the program, you must register in either the Expression of Interest system or the Express Entry system to let the OINP know you wish to be invited to apply.

## After you get a notification of interest through the Express Entry system

If you receive a notification of interest through the Express Entry system, you do not need to contact the program. You must submit your application to the OINP within **45 calendar days** from the date that your [notification of interest](https://www.ontario.ca/page/oinp-express-entry-notifications-interest) from Ontario is issued.

### 1. Find out which draw you were invited under

After you receive a notification of interest, you may submit an online application to the OINP if you qualify under the Ontario Express Entry stream for which your notification of interest was issued. Use the date of your notification of interest to locate the details of the draw on the [OINPProgram Updates page](https://www.ontario.ca/page/2024-ontario-immigrant-nominee-program-updates).

### 2. Make sure you meet the requirements for the draw

Before you apply, make sure you meet the requirements for the draw in which your notification of interest was issued.

### 3. Make sure you meet the requirements for the stream

Each stream has a number of requirements you must meet before you can apply.

Review the stream requirements below:

#### French-Speaking Skilled Worker stream

Apply for the [French-Speaking Skilled Worker stream](https://www.ontario.ca/page/ontarios-express-entry-french-speaking-skilled-worker-stream) if you are a French-speaking skilled worker with strong English language abilities.

#### Human Capital Priorities stream

Apply for the [Human Capital Priorities stream](https://www.ontario.ca/page/ontarios-express-entry-human-capital-priorities-stream) if you have the required skilled work experience, education and language abilities.

#### Skilled Trades stream

Apply for the [Skilled Trades stream](https://www.ontario.ca/page/ontarios-express-entry-skilled-trades-stream) if you are a foreign national with Ontario work experience in an eligible skilled trade.

#### 4. Have your supporting documents ready

You must provide supporting documents to validate the information in your application and to prove you meet the eligibility criteria.

You must scan and upload all supporting documents in your online application. Refer to the [document checklist](https://www.ontario.ca/document/oinp-document-checklists) for the stream under which you wish to apply.

**As of February 26, 2024**, you must sign, date and upload the [Applicant Consent Form](https://forms.mgcs.gov.on.ca/dataset/on00596) with your application. Please complete this form after you have reviewed your application details in the e-filing portal. If you have an immigration representative, they may not sign this form on your behalf.

If a supporting document is not in French or English, you must provide a copy of the original document and a notarized translation of the document. Please read [Document translation and notarization](https://www.ontario.ca/document/oinp-document-checklists/oinp-document-translation-and-notarization) for more information.

Please note that your application must be complete and all required documents must be uploaded. If your application is not complete, including all required documents, we will return your application and refund your fee.

### 5. Submit your application

If you are using a representative, or you are a representative, the submission process is different. Learn more about [how to appoint a representative or register as one](https://www.ontario.ca/page/using-representative-apply-ontario-immigrant-nominee-program).

Follow these steps to apply:

1. Click the submit an application button below.
2. Log into your Ontario.ca Login as a returning user. If you do not have one, click “Create Account”
3. Create a profile in the OINP e-Filing Portal.
4. Click on the file number beside the stream for which you received a notification and begin your application.
[Submit an application](https://www.ontarioimmigration.gov.on.ca/oinp_index/resources/app/guest/index.html#!/)

#### Cost to apply

The application fee is $1,500 and covers the cost of processing your application.

We only accept Visa, Visa Debit, Mastercard and Mastercard Debit.

#### Refunds

We will only refund the fee if we find that your application is incomplete or you withdraw your application before we start processing it.

We will not refund the fee if your application is unsuccessful.

You can check the status of your application through your account in the OINP e-Filing Portal.

### 6. Maintain a valid Express Entry profile

You must have a valid profile in the Express Entry system from when you apply until you are nominated. If your profile expires during the processing of your application, you must notify us as soon as possible via our [webform](https://www.ontarioimmigration.gov.on.ca/oinp_index/resources/app/home/index.html#!/form). Please include both of the following documents:

- a copy of the letter you received from Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) acknowledging the new profile with Express Entry number and job seeker validation code
- a copy of the letter received from Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada explaining why your old profile became ineligible

Failure to notify the program of your new Express Entry profile will result in your application being returned as incomplete and your application fee refunded.

If you receive an Invitation to Apply (ITA) for permanent residence from IRCC while your application is being processed by the OINP, your profile is no longer available for us to nominate you.

You must decide to either:

- reject the ITA issued by IRCC
- [withdraw your application to theOINP](https://www.ontario.ca/page/ontarios-express-entry-system-streams#section-6)

If you reject the ITA you must send us a request to continue processing your application as soon as possible via our [webform](https://www.ontarioimmigration.gov.on.ca/oinp_index/resources/app/home/index.html#!/form) and include a screenshot showing that you

## After you get an invitation to apply through the Expression of Interest ( EOI ) system

If you received an invitation to apply through the Expression of Interest system you do not need to contact the program.

### Masters or PhD Graduate stream deadline

You must submit your application within 14 calendar days from the date you were issued the invitation to apply.

### Employer Job Offer: International student, In-Demand or Foreign Worker stream deadlines

**Your employer’s deadline:** submit the application for the approval of an employment position within 14 calendar days of when we issued the invitation to apply.

**Your deadline as an applicant**: submit your application and pay:

- within 17 calendar days of when we issued the invitation to apply
- after your employer submits their application for approval of the employment position (you can work on your application any time during the 17 days, but cannot submit it until after your employer submits the application for approval of an employment position)

### 1. Make sure you meet the requirements

Each stream has a number of requirements you must meet before you can apply.

Review the stream requirements below:

#### Employer Job Offer: Foreign Worker stream

Apply for the [Employer Job Offer: Foreign Worker stream](https://www.ontario.ca/page/oinp-employer-job-offer-foreign-worker-stream) if you are a skilled foreign worker with a job offer in Ontario or a physician.

#### Employer Job Offer: International Student stream

Apply for the [Employer Job Offer: International Student stream](https://www.ontario.ca/page/oinp-employer-job-offer-international-student-stream) if you are a recent graduate with a job offer in Ontario.

#### Employer Job Offer: In-Demand Skills stream

Apply for the [Employer Job Offer: In Demand Skills stream](https://www.ontario.ca/page/oinp-employer-job-offer-demand-skills-stream) if you are a foreign worker in an in-demand occupation with a job offer in Ontario.

#### Masters Graduate stream

Apply for the [Masters Graduate stream](https://www.ontario.ca/page/oinp-masters-graduate-stream) if you have a master’s degree from an Ontario university.

#### PhD Graduate stream

Apply for the [PhD Graduate stream](https://www.ontario.ca/page/oinp-phd-graduate-stream) if you have a PhD degree from an Ontario university.

### 2. Have your supporting documents ready

You must provide supporting documents to validate the information in your application and to prove you meet the eligibility criteria.

You must scan and upload all supporting documents in your online application. Refer to the [document checklist](https://www.ontario.ca/document/oinp-document-checklists) for the stream under which you wish to apply.

**As of February 26, 2024**, you must sign, date and upload the [Applicant Consent Form](https://forms.mgcs.gov.on.ca/dataset/on00596) with your application. Please complete this form after you have reviewed your application details in the e-filing portal. If you have an immigration representative, they may not sign this form on your behalf.

If a supporting document is not in French or English, you must provide a copy of the original document and a notarized translation of the document. Please read [Document translation and notarization](https://www.ontario.ca/document/oinp-document-checklists/oinp-document-translation-and-notarization) for more information.

Please note that your application must be complete and all required documents must be uploaded. If your application is not complete, including all required documents, we will return your application and refund your fee.

### 3. Submit your application

If you are using a representative, or you are a representative, the submission process is different. Learn more about [how to appoint a representative or register as one](https://www.ontario.ca/page/using-representative-apply-ontario-immigrant-nominee-program).

Follow these steps to apply:

1. Click the “Submit an application” button below.
2. Log into your Ontario.ca Login as a returning user.
3. From your My Applications page, click on the file number link to begin your application.

Your new application file number is different than your EOI file number. The EOI file number (EOI-JOXX-XXXXXX) will be greyed out — this is not an error. Do not withdraw your EOI file.

You must demonstrate that you meet all stream requirements at the time of your application, **including** that you had the qualifications that you claimed in your expression of interest registration.

If we determine that any information provided in your expression of interest registration is not accurate, is incorrect, or misleading, your application may be denied, and you may be subject to penalties or prosecution.
[Submit an application](https://www.ontarioimmigration.gov.on.ca/oinp_index/resources/app/guest/index.html#!/)

#### If you receive an invitation but do not apply

If you receive an invitation to apply but do not submit an application or fail to apply within the required timeline, your expression of interest will be removed from the stream selection pool (expressions of interest you may have registered for other streams will not be removed).

You will receive an email notification that your expression of interest has been removed from the selection pool. To register a new expression of interest, your employer will first need to submit a new job offer.

#### Cost to apply

For the Masters Graduate, PhD Graduate, Employer Job Offer: International Student streams, and physicians who are eligible to apply to the Employer Job Offer: Foreign Worker stream without a job offer, the application fee is $1,500.

For the Employer Job Offer: Foreign Worker and In-Demand Skills streams, the application fee is as follows:

- $1,500 if the job offer is outside of the Greater Toronto Area
- $2,000 if the job offer is within the Greater Toronto Area (City of Toronto, Durham, Halton, York and Peel regions)

The fee covers the cost of processing your application. We only accept Visa, Visa Debit, Mastercard and Mastercard Debit.

#### Refunds

We will only refund the fee if we find that your application is incomplete or you withdraw your application before we start processing it.

We will not refund the fee if your application is unsuccessful.

You can check the status of your application through your account in the [OINPe-Filing Portal](https://www.ontarioimmigration.gov.on.ca/oinp_index/resources/app/guest/index.html#!/).

#### Incomplete Application

If your application is deemed incomplete for any reason, it will be withdrawn by the OINP and processing fees will be returned to you. It will not be possible to request an internal review. An Internal Review is only available to applicants whose applications have been assessed against applicable eligibility criteria and in which a decision has been reached. If the application is deemed incomplete, it has not yet been assessed against applicable eligibility criteria and hence a decision has not been made in this regard.
