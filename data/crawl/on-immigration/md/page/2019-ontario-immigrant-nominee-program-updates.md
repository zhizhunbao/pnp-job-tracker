### December 20, 2019

#### Program Update: Regional Immigration Pilot

The Ontario Immigrant Nominee Program (OINP) will begin a two-year Regional Immigration Pilot in early 2020. The Regional Immigration Pilot will help fill labour market needs of businesses in small and rural communities that are not currently being met locally.

While developing the pilot, feedback was gathered from stakeholders in a number of small and rural communities across the province. Communities were chosen based on their specific skilled labour challenges, the ability to help newcomers get settled, and interest from community stakeholders.

The communities selected for the Regional Immigration Pilot are:

- **Chatham-Kent** – a unique rural and small urban environment for newcomers and a highly integrated “welcoming community”.
- **Cornwall** – an opportunity to work with a very engaged Francophone community with high employment needs for English and French employers.
- **Belleville/Quinte West** – a regional approach to an area with a high-level of regional engagement and coordination among economic development and immigration stakeholders

The OINP will work collaboratively with local stakeholders in the three pilot communities to spread awareness of the OINP and increase program uptake. This work could include in-person training sessions with local employers, informational webinars or OINP attendance at local industry or employer events/conferences.

The pilot will also include a set-aside of approximately 150 nominations under the OINP Employer Job Offer Category streams, which will be reserved for applicants with job offers in the pilot communities.

Outcomes from the pilot will help inform further efforts to regionalize economic immigration in Ontario.

The OINP will also continue to engage with all communities in Ontario to raise awareness and understanding of the Ontario Immigrant Nominee Program and how the program could help fill local labour market gaps.

More information about the Regional Immigration Pilot can be found [here](https://www.ontario.ca/page/regional-immigration-pilot).

### November 4, 2019

ALERT: The OINP Entrepreneur Stream is administered in accordance with the requirements set out in the Ontario Immigration Act, 2015 and the regulations made thereunder. A passive investment option is NOT permitted under the Entrepreneur Stream. While passive investments, including mutual and/or pooled fund securities, are permissible for the purpose of satisfying the personal net worth criterion of the Entrepreneur Stream, they are NOT eligible for inclusion in an applicant’s minimum business investment amount. Neither the Government of Ontario nor the Ontario Immigrant Nominee Program (OINP) has approved or endorsed any foreign investment program or fund, whether designed to facilitate the nomination of a foreign national for permanent residence in Ontario, or otherwise. Any claim otherwise is false.

## July

### July 15, 2019

#### Ontario Regional Immigration Pilot

Ontario is open for business and a skilled labour force is at the core of the province’s economy.

The Ontario government is working to make it easier for businesses to attract top talent and skilled workers from around the world.

To spread the benefits of immigration to smaller communities, Ministry of Economic Development, Job Creation and Trade staff will work with communities to explore innovative approaches to bring highly skilled immigrants to smaller communities.

The government is engaging with selected rural and small community stakeholders to assess interest in the proposed pilot and to gather information about existing community immigrant attraction and retention efforts.

It is anticipated that the pilot would begin in 2020 and over the term of the pilot the government would work closely with local stakeholders to support their efforts to retain and attract skilled labour to their communities.

#### Regional Pilot Highlights

The regional immigration pilot is intended to be a made-in-Ontario approach to bring skilled workers to rural and smaller communities to fill labour gaps.

The government is currently gathering feedback on pilot development from a geographically and culturally diverse sample of communities across Ontario.

Through engagement with community municipal partners and key stakeholders, the government will determine local interest and capacity for participation in the pilot.

Statistical analysis was used to identify prospective communities using the following criteria: population size (20,000 to 200,000), newcomer settlement, newcomer settlement infrastructure, such as language and settlement services, and economic characteristics.

Final selection of pilot communities would be based on data analysis, along with the results from engagement with communities.

Ontario’s regional immigration pilot is not intended to duplicate the Federal Rural and Northern Immigration Pilot. An Ontario pilot would specifically target the needs of Ontario communities and explore how to better regionalize the OINP to better support Ontario employers in smaller communities.

### July 8, 2019

#### Important Notice - Regulatory Amendments

Please be advised that the regulations under the Ontario Immigration Act, 2015 have been amended. New applications will be processed in accordance with the amended regulations posted on e-Laws on July 8, 2019.
 Amendments were made to simplify program requirements, add greater clarity and consistency to stream criteria, and introduce changes to the In-Demand Skills Stream and the Entrepreneur Stream. The amendments include the following:

- Under the In-Demand Skills Stream, the work experience requirement has been reduced and three new National Occupational Classification (NOC) codes related to personal support workers and transport truck drivers were added as eligible occupations to support changing labour market needs.
- To make the Entrepreneur Stream more attractive to job creators, and compete with comparable jurisdictions, the threshold of investment needed has been lowered from $1 million to $600,000 in the Greater Toronto Area (GTA) and from $500,000 to $200,000 outside the GTA or in the information and communications technology or digital communications sector. The net worth thresholds have also been reduced and the applicant screening process has been fine-tuned to ensure Ontario is attracting the best businesses and entrepreneurs.

Program materials, including application guides and website pages, have been updated to reflect the regulatory amendments. Carefully review the relevant application guide to ensure you meet all stream criteria before submitting an application.

We encourage you to review the updated regulations on Ontario’s e-Laws website:

- [Ontario Regulation 422/17](https://www.ontario.ca/laws/regulation/170422) (General)
- [Ontario Regulation 421/17](https://www.ontario.ca/laws/regulation/170421) (Approvals under the Ontario Immigrant Nominee Program and Other Matters)

Demand for the Ontario Immigrant Nominee Program (OINP) is strong. The province’s nomination allocation from the federal government was 6,600 in 2018.

The OINP met its full 2018 nomination allocation in early November. In December, the federal government granted Ontario an additional 250 nominations, which were allocated before year end. This brought the total number of nominations in 2018 to 6,850 principal applicants. Those applications included 6,721 spouses and dependents for a total of 13,571 new immigrants to Ontario.

Nominations were issued under the following streams:

| Stream | Number of Nominations |
| --- | --- |
| Employer Job Offer: International Student Stream | 1,254 |
| Employer Job Offer: In-Demand Skills Stream | 35 |
| Employer Job Offer: Foreign Worker Stream | 934 |
| PhD Graduate Stream | 175 |
| Masters Graduate Stream | 1,215 |
| Ontario’s Express Entry Skilled Trades Stream | 648 |
| Ontario’s Express Entry Human Capital Priorities Stream | 2,190 |
| Ontario’s Express Entry French-Speaking Skilled Worker Stream | 387 |
| Business Streams footnote 2 [2] | 12 |
| Grand Total | 6,850 |

In 2018, the OINP saw a high number of nominations in technology-related occupations. Approximately 25 per cent of all nominations were to individuals citing work experience or a job offer in a high-tech occupation, including software engineers and designers, computer programmers, interactive media developers, IT systems analysts and technology consultants.

The program also surpassed its 5 per cent target for francophone immigration, reaching a program high of 7.7 per cent or 526 francophone nominees. Most applicants received nominations under the French-Speaking Skilled Worker Stream and the Human Capital Priorities Stream.

Nominees in 2018 came from over 140 different countries with applicants from India and China receiving the highest number of nominations.

At least 36 per cent of nominees already had a job offer in Ontario prior to nomination. More than 94 per cent held a post-secondary degree and at least 60 per cent had high language skills (CLB 7 or above) in at least one of Canada’s official languages.

The OINP will continue to be responsive to Ontario employers and to Ontario’s broader labour market needs in 2019 and beyond. This includes continuous improvements to program delivery, customer service, as well as incorporating stakeholder feedback to help address labour shortages in high-demand sectors.

**Note:** Ontario is currently awaiting its nomination allocation for 2019 from the federal government.
