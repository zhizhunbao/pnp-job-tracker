## Overview

Immigration New Brunswick (ImmigrationNB) uses an expression of interest (EOI) system to manage the intake of immigration applications for the New Brunswick Provincial Nominee Program (NBPNP). Before you can submit a complete application, you must submit an EOI in the INB portal.

Submitted EOIs are placed in the candidate pool and remain valid for 365 days from the date of submission. Be sure to [keep your EOI up to date](https://www2.gnb.ca/content/gnb/en/corporate/promo/immigration/immigrating-to-nb/nb-immigration-program-streams/candidate-pool.html#3).

ImmigrationNB will review these EOIs and issue invitations to apply (ITAs) based on provincial labour market needs, available allocation, and other factors and priorities determined by the Government of New Brunswick.

Meeting the eligibility requirements for an NBPNP stream does not guarantee that you will be invited to apply. Moreover, receiving an ITA does not guarantee that you will be nominated.

If you are invited to apply, you must submit a complete application under the stream specified in your invitation and within the timeframe indicated in the invitation.

## Create a candidate profile

Register on the INB portal and create a candidate profile. Always keep your contact information up to date.

**Preparation**  You will need:  your personal email account; and your passport.

**How to register and create a profile**  1. Go to the [INB portal](https://inb.gnb.ca/)  Click on **Registration** Enter your given names and surname (family name) *exactly as they are shown on your passport* If you don’t have a surname (family name) on your passport, enter all your names in the **Given name** field and put **NA** in the **Family name** field   Enter your *personal* email address Create a password Click on the **Register** button   2. Create your profile and provide information about yourself  Your personal details Enter your given names and surname (family name) *exactly as they are shown on your passport* If you don’t have a surname (family name) on your passport, enter all your names in the **Given names as shown on passport** field and leave the **Family name as shown as passport** field blank Enter your birthdate in the format: YYYY-MM-DD   Your citizenship Your family composition Your contact information   3. Review all your details to ensure the information is correct, then click the **Save** button

**Keep in mind** You cannot edit or change your name or birthdate after you save your profile. Be very careful not to make mistakes. To request a correction to your name or your birthdate, [contact us](https://www2.gnb.ca/content/gnb/en/corporate/promo/immigration/contact/candidate.html) with your request and include in your email a copy of the biodata page of your passport for verification. You can edit or change information relating to your citizenship, family composition, or contact information by clicking on the “Edit” button at the top of your profile page.

> Important
> To ensure you always have full access to your INB profile, follow these best practice tips: create your own profile using your own *personal* email address and password; never share your INB login information with anyone.   If a third party created an account on your behalf, demand to have the login information to verify the documents yourself. If you do not have access to the email address used to create an INB profile, we may not be able to grant you access to that profile, even if it exists in your name.

## Submit an expression of interest

Explore available [immigration streams and pathways](https://www2.gnb.ca/content/gnb/en/corporate/promo/immigration/immigrating-to-nb/nb-immigration-program-streams.html) and decide on one stream you believe you are eligible for and will have the best chance at, then submit your expression of interest (EOI).

**Preparation**  You will need:  your work permit or study permit, if applicable; valid language test scores, if applicable; information about your employment in New Brunswick, if applicable; and the NB-004 Employment Information form, signed and completed by your supporting employer (required for all pathways of the New Brunswick Skilled Worker stream and the New Brunswick Private Career College Graduate Pilot).

**How to submit an EOI**   Log in to your INB account. Click the “Start” button on your Dashboard page. Select your immigration stream. Complete the questionnaire. Submit your EOI.  Each stream of the NBPNP may have several different pathways. Your answers to the questionnaire will allow ImmigrationNB to determine which pathways of that stream you may qualify for.

**Multiple EOIs or applications**An INB profile can have only one active EOI or application. If you have an EOI in the NBPNP candidate pool and you want to submit an EOI for different programs or streams, you can:  withdraw your current EOI and submit a new one  **or**  keep your current EOI and create a separate INB profile using a different email address to submit an additional EOI  If you want to submit an AIP endorsement application to the [AIP candidate pool](https://www2.gnb.ca/content/gnb/en/corporate/promo/immigration/immigrating-to-nb/atlantic-immigration-program/aip-candidate-pool.html) and also submit an EOI to the NBPNP candidate pool, you must create a separate INB profile for each program. You can only have one active application with ImmigrationNB. This means you can be in both the NBPNP and AIP candidate pools, but you can only move forward with one of them.  If you receive an invitation to apply (ITA) for an NBPNP stream, you must withdraw all your other submissions If your AIP endorsement application is selected for processing, you must withdraw all your EOIs If you receive an ITA for an NBPNP stream and your AIP endorsement application is selected for processing, you must choose only one and withdraw the other  If you submit more than one active application, **all of them could be refused.**

## Update an expression of interest

Be sure to update your expression of interest (EOI) if any of the information you provided has changed.
**How to update an EOI**

To make changes to your EOI, simply withdraw your current EOI and submit a new one with the appropriate changes.

These changes may be, but are not limited to, any of the following:

- a new employer;
- a new NOC code;
- new work permit details;
- new federal Express Entry profile number and job seeker validation code;
- correction to any answers provided;
- changing from one immigration stream to another.

EOIs are not considered on a first come, first served basis. Withdrawing and resubmitting an EOI does not place you at the back of the queue.

## NBPNP candidate pool

Submitted EOIs are placed in the candidate pool, and they remain valid for 365 days from the date of submission.

**Invitations to apply**  ImmigrationNB reviews submitted EOIs and issues invitations to apply (ITAs) only to selected individuals through scheduled invitation draws. The submission of an EOI does not guarantee that you will receive an ITA.  See the [latest and previous invitations.](https://www2.gnb.ca/content/gnb/en/corporate/promo/immigration/immigrating-to-nb/invitations-to-apply.html)

> Important
> An EOI is not a complete application to the province. An EOI cannot provide support for an expiring work permit. You are responsible for [maintaining your authorization to work](https://www2.gnb.ca/content/gnb/en/corporate/promo/immigration/immigrating-to-nb/maintain-your-status.html) throughout the immigration process.

## Submit a complete application

If you receive an invitation to apply (ITA), you will submit a complete application to ImmigrationNB and pay the processing fee through the INB portal within the timeframe indicated in the invitation. If you do not do so before the deadline, your ITA and the associated EOI will automatically expire. After expiry, you will need to start over from the beginning by submitting a new expression of interest if you wish to have further consideration from the province. ImmigrationNB cannot guarantee that you will be invited again.

Receiving an ITA does not guarantee a nomination. You are responsible for ensuring that you meet all eligibility requirements before applying.

Submitting a complete application means you will provide more details about yourself (and about your spouse or common-law partner and any dependent, if applicable), as well as all of the required supporting documents.

Your chosen program or stream cannot be changed once you have applied. Additionally, you can have only one active application with ImmigrationNB at any given moment. If you have more than one active applications, you will be asked to choose one and withdraw the rest. Any processing fee paid for withdrawn applications will not be refunded.
