If you are a newcomer living in Manitoba and are looking for newcomer services, and have permanent resident status or meet other specific federal criteria, please visit IRCC’s webpage [here](https://ircc.canada.ca/english/newcomers/services/index.asp).

If you are not eligible for IRCC funded settlement services and meet below eligibility, please see [here](https://immigratemanitoba.com/ncis/msss/) for a service directory of Manitoba Settlement Support Services (MSSS) funded services that may be able to help you.

You are eligible to receive MSSS-funded services if you reside in Manitoba and you are:

- A temporary resident holding a valid work permit or study permit
- A Canadian citizen who identifies as a newcomer, including naturalized citizens, Canadian-born citizens raised outside of Canada, and foreign-born children of a Canadian citizen
- A refugee claimant  Only claimants with a valid work permit are eligible for employment-related services Among refugee claimants, claimants with a valid work permit will be prioritized under MSSS services categories other than employment related services.
- The spouse/common-law partner and dependent children of the above

Manitoba Start is the first step for all newcomers to the province. Learn what they offer [here](https://manitobastart.com/). [Accueil francophone](https://accueilfrancophonemb.com/) is an alternative for French-speaking newcomers.
