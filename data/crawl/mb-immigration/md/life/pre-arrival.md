## Working in Manitoba

Whether you have a job arranged before you arrive, or plan to find work after you arrive:

- Learn as much as you can about the labour market in Manitoba.
- Research your occupation or the industries that you are interested in.
- Consider how your new job (workplace culture and duties) may be different in Manitoba.
- Manitoba has plenty of job opportunities and resources available to help newcomers understand requirements for working in Manitoba. See [Newcomer Fact Sheet](https://www.gov.mb.ca/asset_library/en/manitoba4ukraine/newcomer-factsheet.pdf).
- A wide range of employment and training services are offered through 13[Manitoba Jobs and Skills Development Centres](https://www.gov.mb.ca/wd/ites/tes/jobs_careers.html) situated throughout Manitoba, as well as through partnerships with community-based organizations and employers. Anyone authorized to work in Canada are eligible for general services. Services may include employment counselling, help to develop an employment plan, access to information about occupations and training opportunities, and financial supports.
- The[Welcome to Canada app](https://win.manitobastart.com/welcome-to-canada/) will help ease your settlement journey by connecting you to information and services in your area. Based on anonymous questionnaire responses and geographic location, you will receive customized recommendations. Discover the information and services you need to learn English, seek employment, find housing, explore education opportunities and more.
- Research employment opportunities using websites such as [Canada Job Bank](https://www.jobbank.gc.ca/trend-analysis/search-occupations), [Work in Manitoba Jobs Portal](https://workinmb.poweredbymagnet.ca/), [Manitoba Start Job postings](https://manitobastart.com/job-postings/#ukrainian-initiative), [Workopolis](https://www.workopolis.com/), and [Indeed](https://ca.indeed.com/).
- If you work in a [regulated profession or trade](https://immigratemanitoba.com/life/regulated-occupations/), learn about your occupation and the requirements to work in Manitoba.  Visit the website of the professional regulatory body specific to your regulated profession or trade. There are licensing/certification steps you may be able to complete before you arrive.
- Familiarize yourself with the Canadian style of job searching techniques, resumes, interviews and hiring practices.
- If you already have a job offer, learn about your new employer or/the company you will be working with.
- Practice listening, speaking, reading, and writing as much as possible in the language you will use when you live and work in Manitoba. See [English and French in Manitoba](https://immigratemanitoba.com/life/official-languages/).

## Prepare to live in Manitoba

Learn as much as you can before arriving about the facilities, available services, culture, climate and geography of the community that you will live in.

- Learn about Manitoba and the city or town in which you plan to live.
- Learn about the [weather](https://weather.gc.ca/forecast/canada/index_e.html), particularly in the month of your arrival.
- Be certain you have a [place to live for the first few weeks](https://www.travelmanitoba.com/stay/) until you can get yourself settled permanently.
- Gather and bring important documentation related to work and school. Examples include:  letters of reference from employers high school diploma and transcript college/university diplomas and transcripts professional certification/licenses

Travel Manitoba has a selection of videos showcasing the places and people of Manitoba on their [YouTube channel](https://www.youtube.com/user/TravelManitoba) and [website](https://www.travelmanitoba.com/). Tourism Winnipeg also has videos of Winnipeg on their [YouTube channel](https://www.youtube.com/user/tourismwpg).

## Pre-arrival services

These pre-arrival services are **free **and available to you if you have received a Confirmation of Permanent Residence (COPR) **or** a notification about a positive eligibility decision on your application from IRCC, and you are outside of Canada.

- [Active Engagement and Integration Program (AEIP)](https://aeipsuccess.ca/)
- [English Online](https://english-online.ca/)
- [Planning for Canada](https://www.planningforcanada.ca/)
