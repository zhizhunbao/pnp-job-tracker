## Expression of Interest (EOI) Validity Period and Transition Measures
April 27, 2026 | Effective May 1, 2026, Nova Scotia will introduce a 12‑month validity period for Expressions of Interest (EOIs) as part of improved inventory management and program efficiency measures. Nova Scotia offers a variety of free mental health support services to people in need.

**Mental Health Crisis Line**
A 24/7 helpline offering immediate support for people in mental health crises.
**Call 1-888-429-8167**

**Nova Scotia Health: Mental Health and Addictions Services**
This website includes descriptions of available services, how to access help, health information, and links to access the following online, self-directed mental health supports.
[mha.nshealth.ca](https://mha.nshealth.ca/en)

**Suicide Crisis Helpline**
A three-digit helpline that provides urgent, live support by phone and text for people in Canada who are thinking about suicide or worried about someone else who may be thinking about suicide. It is available in English and French, 24 hours a day and seven days a week, to everyone in Canada.
**Call 9-8-8**

### 211 Community and Social Services

211 is a service offered in Nova Scotia to help residents find community and social services. By dialing 211 on your phone or visiting the 211 website, users will easily and quickly be connected to the community and social services they need, wherever they are in the province. 211 offers interpretation services for over 100 languages. [Learn more](http://www.ns.211.ca/).

The Government of Nova Scotia is working with the YMCA to support Ukrainian newcomers arriving in Nova Scotia. We’re also streamlining the immigration process for Ukrainians who want to move here.

### Support for Ukrainian Newcomers

The YMCA of Halifax has [information for Ukrainians settling in Nova Scotia](https://ymcahfx.ca/nssupportsukriane/), including information about finding housing and jobs, accessing healthcare, taking language classes and enrolling children in school.

### Immigration Programs

There are several immigration programs available to help you settle in Nova Scotia. Contact one of our community navigators to [learn more about available programs](https://liveinnovascotia.com/contact).

### Immigration Information Sessions

We hosted an online information sessions about how Ukrainians can immigrate to Nova Scotia. The sessions included information from the Government of Canada about temporary federal measures for Ukrainians coming to Canada.

We’ll post information about the sessions on our website, on [Facebook](https://www.facebook.com/NSimmigration/) and on [Twitter](https://twitter.com/NSimmigration/). [See all our contact information.](https://liveinnovascotia.com/contact)

To receive information about our information sessions, check out our [Events page.](https://liveinnovascotia.com/events)

### Federal Government Programs and Services

[Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada has information about immigration measures for people affected by the Russian invasion of Ukraine](https://www.canada.ca/en/immigration-refugees-citizenship/services/immigrate-canada/ukraine-measures.html).

### How Nova Scotians Can Help

[The YMCA has information for Nova Scotians](https://ymcahfx.ca/ns-supports-ukraine/wanting-to-help/) who want to help Ukrainian newcomers by making a donation, offering housing or offering a job.
