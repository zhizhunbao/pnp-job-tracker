Once you're ready to move to beautiful Nova Scotia you will want to submit an expression of interest (EOI) to a Nova Scotia Nominee Program (NSNP) stream. Through the NSNP, prospective immigrants who have the skills and experience needed by Nova Scotia employers may be nominated to immigrate.

Review the different NSNP streams to determine if you qualify.

> **UPDATE: April 27, 2026**
> Nova Scotia has introduced a 12-month validity period for Expressions of Interest (EOIs), effective May 1, 2026. Transition measures apply to EOIs currently in the pool. Learn more: [EOI validity period and transition measures](https://liveinnovascotia.com/resources/expression-interest-eoi-validity-period-and-transition-measures) · See also: [Current selection priorities](https://liveinnovascotia.com/resources/nominee-program-priorities-nova-scotia) and: [EOI Selection](https://liveinnovascotia.com/eoi-selection)

> **UPDATE: February 5, 2026**
> The Nova Scotia Nominee Program (NSNP) is modernizing its forms to improve efficiency and support the launch of consolidated NSNP streams. To complete these updates, the NSNP application portal will be temporarily closed from February 13 to February 18, 2026. [Read the update here](https://liveinnovascotia.com/stream-modern-temp-portal-closure).

> **UPDATE: November 28, 2025**
> Nova Scotia is formalizing an expression of interest (EOI) process to remain responsive to labour market needs and support sustainable, long-term community growth. [Read the update here.](https://liveinnovascotia.com/eoi-process)

[Within twelve (12)](https://www.canada.ca/en/immigration-refugees-citizenship/services/immigrate-canada/provincial-nominees.html) months of receiving your nominee certificate, apply to Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) for your permanent resident visa. You, your spouse and your dependents must meet all requirements for medical, security and criminal admissibility. In some cases you may be asked to go for an interview. IRCC has the final authority to issue a permanent resident visa.

Additional information on provincial nominees can be [found here](https://www.canada.ca/en/immigration-refugees-citizenship/services/immigrate-canada/provincial-nominees.html).
