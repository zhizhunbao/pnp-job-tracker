[Office of Immigration and Multiculturalism](https://www.gov.nl.ca/immigration)Immigrating to Newfoundland and Labrador

Newfoundland and Labrador has two immigration programs for skilled workers, international graduates, and entrepreneurs to settle here with their families and gain permanent residence. These programs also help local employers find workers.

**The Newfoundland and Labrador Provincial Nominee Program (NLPNP)**

- Nominates people for permanent residence.
- Is a quick process for skilled workers, graduates, and entrepreneurs.
- Gives newcomers and employers support from Immigration Program Development Officers.

For more details, visit:

[Information for NLPNP applicants](https://www.gov.nl.ca/immigration/immigrating-to-newfoundland-and-labrador/provincial-nominee-program/overview/)

[Information for employers interested in the NLPNP](https://www.gov.nl.ca/immigration/immigrating-to-newfoundland-and-labrador/provincial-nominee-program/employers/overview/)

**The Atlantic Immigration Program (AIP)**

- Helps employers hire skilled international workers and graduates.
- Focuses on employer engagement, settlement support, and collaboration across Atlantic Provinces.
- Is not available for employers recruiting for low-skilled (TEER 5) occupations.

For more details, visit:

[Information for workers interested in learning more about the AIP](https://www.gov.nl.ca/immigration/immigrating-to-newfoundland-and-labrador/atlantic-immigration-program/workers/)

[Information for employers interested in applying to the AIP](https://www.gov.nl.ca/immigration/immigrating-to-newfoundland-and-labrador/atlantic-immigration-program/employers/)
