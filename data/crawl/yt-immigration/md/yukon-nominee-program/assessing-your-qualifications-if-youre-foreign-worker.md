A foreign worker can expect questions about their education and work experience from employers, immigration officers or academic institutions. Be prepared by having records of your qualifications and credentials.
The difference between credentials and qualifications 

### Foreign credentials

These documents are issued outside Canada and show that you have completed an educational program, such as school diplomas, academic transcripts and occupational certifications.

### Foreign qualifications

Similar to foreign credentials, these documents show work experience. Some people use the term foreign qualifications and foreign credentials to mean the same thing.
​Regulated occupations 

In regulated occupations, you need to be certified and registered by a regulatory authority that establishes and upholds occupational standards. Each profession requires different standards for entering practice.

Non-regulated occupations 

Please refer to the [ESDC website](https://www.jobbank.gc.ca/intro) for minimum requirements for non-regulated occupations. Also check the employer’s expectations for a job. Employers determine whether your qualifications will match the requirements of a specific job. The employer may ask for an independent assessment of your educational credentials or past work experience.
Internationally educated health professionals 

We offer the Foreign Credential Recognition Navigation Service for internationally educated health professionals. We can help you access services to get your foreign credentials recognized to work in health care in the Yukon.

Learn more about the [Foreign Credential Recognition Navigation Service](https://yukon.ca/en/doing-business/professional-licensing/get-your-foreign-credentials-recognized-work-health-care), including:

- eligibility;
- services we offer;
- credentials you need;
- participant guidelines; and
- contact information.
Where can you get your foreign credentials assessed? 

Assessment agencies provide employment and educational evaluation services and compare them to credentials in Canada. For more information, visit any of the following websites:

- [International Credential Evaluation Service (ICES)](http://www.bcit.ca/ices/)
- [World Education Services (WES) *Service available in English and French](http://www.wes.org/ca/application/apply_now.asp)
- [International Credential Assessment Service of Canada](http://www.icascanada.ca/home.aspx)
