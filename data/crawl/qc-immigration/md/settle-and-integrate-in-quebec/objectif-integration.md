# Objectif Intégration Information Session for Immigrants

The Objectif Intégration information session allows you to familiarize yourself with Québec’s values and cultural and democratic norms and the world of work in Quebec. The session is intended for immigrants who have an intermediate knowledge of French.

## On this page:

## Description of the Objectif Intégration Session

### Free of charge

Participation in the session is free.

### Location

The session is given by several partner organizations of the Gouvernement du Québec, throughout Québec.

### Language

The session is offered in French only.

### Session duration

The overall length of the session is 24 hours.

### Contents of the session

The session covers:

- Democratic values and the Québec values expressed in the *Charter of Human Rights and Freedoms*
- Cultural norms in the workplace
- The legal framework in the workplace

The content of the session is divided into three sections:

- Section 1: Diversity and Adaptation in Québec
- Section 2: Five Keys to Better Understand Québec
- Section 3: The World of Work in Québec

### Attestation

As of January 1, 2020, you must demonstrate your knowledge of democratic values and the Québec values expressed in the *Charter of Human Rights and Freedoms* if you apply to one of these programs:

- Programme de sélection des travailleurs qualifiés (Skilled Worker Selection Program)
- Programme de l’expérience québécoise (Québec Experience Program)
- Programme régulier des travailleurs qualifiés (Regular Skilled Worker Program) (since the program endend on November 29, 2024, it applies only to accompanying persons who are included in an application for addition)
- Permanent immigration pilot program for workers in food processing (since the program endend on January 1, 2026, it applies only to accompanying persons who are included in an application for addition)
- Permanent immigration pilot program for orderlies (since the program endend on January 1, 2026, it applies only to accompanying persons who are included in an application for addition)
- Permanent immigration pilot program for workers in the artificial intelligence, information technologies and visual effects sectors (since the program endend on January 1, 2026, it applies only to accompanying persons who are included in an application for addition)
- Entrepreneur Program
- Self-Employed Worker Program
- Investor Program

The members of your family accompanying you must also demonstrate their knowledge of Québec values.

Depending on your [situation at the time of application for permanent selection](https://www.quebec.ca/en/immigration/receiving-attestation-values), you may be able to participate in the Objectif Intégration session to demonstrate your knowledge of the values.

After participating in a full session of Objectif Intégration, you will receive an attestation of participation and learning about democratic values and the Québec values expressed in the *Charter of Human Rights and Freedoms*.

The attestation will be valid for a period of two years.

## Conditions for Admission

To participate in the Objectif Intégration session, you must meet the following conditions.

### Age

You must be 18 years of age or older.

### Knowledge of French

You must have an intermediate knowledge of French to participate in the session.

### Residency in Québec

Immigrants must reside in Quebec to be able to register and participate in the Objectif Intégration session.

### Status for eligibility

You must meet one of the following conditions:

- You are a naturalized Canadian citizen.
- You are a permanent resident of Canada.
- You are a temporary resident and hold a permit issued for the purpose of eventual permanent residence or a permit from the Minister of Immigration, Francization and Integration.
- You are a person authorized to submit an application for permanent residence in Canada.
- You are in Québec as a temporary worker for one or more consecutive periods of one year or more.
- You are a foreign student in Québec for a period of more than six months.
- You are a protected person in Canada (who has been granted refugee protection).
- You hold a tourist visa and you have received an invitation from the Ministère de l'Immigration, de la Francisation et de l'Intégration (the Ministry) to apply for permanent selection.

### If you are working or studying temporarily in Québec

Make sure you abide by the conditions of your status.

If you do not abide by these conditions, you may:

- Lose your status.
- Be refused subsequent entry into Québec or elsewhere in Canada.

For more information, contact [Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada](https://www.canada.ca/en/immigration-refugees-citizenship/corporate/contact-ircc.html).

## How to Register

You can register for the Objectif Intégration session:

With an integration assistance agent (IAA) as part of the Accompagnement Québec [integration service for immigrants](https://www.quebec.ca/en/immigration/integration-service-for-immigrants).

At a [partner organization of the Gouvernement du Québec that offers the training](https://www.quebec.ca/en/immigration/partners). Certain organizations offer a drop-in daycare during the session.

By using the [Ministry’s online appointment scheduling service](https://www.quebec.ca/en/immigration/settle-and-integrate-in-quebec/objectif-integration/online-appointment-scheduling-service).

For more assistance, contact the Ministry’s [Centre de contacts clientèle (information service)](https://immigration-quebec.gouv.qc.ca/en/reach/index.html).

## Financial Assistance

The Gouvernement du Québec offers financial assistance to encourage you to attend the Objectif Intégration session and to cover the attendance fees.

You can receive $230 in financial assistance to participate in the session. You must attend the entire session and meet the eligibility requirements.

This financial assistance is taxable and provided only once to each person.

You will receive payment by bank cheque. The Ministry will make the payment as quickly as possible.

### Conditions for receiving financial assistance

You must:

- Be 18 years of age or older.
- Have attended the entire Objectif Intégration session.
- Have a valid Social Insurance Number (SIN), Individual Tax Number (ITN) or Temporary Tax Number (TTN).
- Be in one of the following situations: You are a naturalized Canadian citizen.You are a resident or a permanent resident of Canada.You are a temporary resident and hold a permit issued for the purpose of eventual permanent residence or a permit from the Minister of Immigration, Francization and Integration.You are a person authorized to submit an application for permanent residence in Canada.You are in Québec as a temporary worker for one or more consecutive periods of one year or more.You are a foreign student in Québec for a period of more than six months.You are a protected person in Canada (who has been granted refugee protection).

### Financial assistance exclusions

You are not eligible to receive financial assistance for your participation in the Objectif Intégration session if you are in one of these situations:

- You have applied for asylum and are awaiting a decision from Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) or the Immigration and Refugee Board (IRB).
- You hold a tourist visa.
- You are no longer allowed to reside in Canada.

### How to apply for financial assistance

To apply for financial assistance, you must:

- Access the online form to [apply for aid to participate in the Objectif Intégration session](https://arrima.immigration-quebec.gouv.qc.ca/Allocation-OI) (in French only).
- Enter the information requested on the form.
- Attach the required document (proof of status).
- Click on the “Soumettre” button at the bottom of the page.
