  About the Role  
 The Office Manager is the heartbeat of Solace’s Kanata headquarters.  This is a full-time, fully onsite contract role (Monday to Friday) , with the possibility of conversion to a permanent position. 
   
 We’re looking for someone who takes genuine pride in owning the office environment. You are warm, organized, and proactive. You are excited to be the first face people see when they walk through the door. 
   
 You will be the go-to person for everything related to the physical workplace: facilities, supplies, vendor relationships, and health and safety. You will also own Solace’s co-op recruitment coordination, working closely with the HR team to ensure a smooth experience for student candidates. 
   
 If you love keeping things running, making people feel welcome, and will never say “that’s not my job”, then this role is for you. 
   
  What You’ll Manage    
  Office Experience &amp; Workplace Welcome  
 
 Serve as the primary greeter and first point of contact for all staff, visitors, and guests. You set a consistently warm, professional tone at the front desk 
 Own the day-to-day office experience at Solace HQ, proactively identifying and resolving anything that affects the look, feel, or function of the workspace 
 Bring energy and positivity to the workplace; come up with creative, thoughtful ways to make the office environment welcoming and fun for everyone 
 Act as the central point of contact for all office-related questions and inquiries from staff and visitors 
 Partner with the HR and People team to support a positive, inclusive employee experience at Solace 
 Prepare and assemble swag bags for new hires and help get them settled in on their first day, ensuring a warm and organized welcome 
 Coordinate food and beverage ordering for department and company events, managing logistics from selection to delivery 
 
   
  Facilities &amp; Space Management  
 
 Coordinate major workstation reconfigurations, moves, and construction projects, including supervising contractors and scheduling with internal stakeholders 
 Research, propose, and implement new office solutions that continuously improve the workplace experience 
 Take ownership of kitchen cleanliness and overall office tidiness, ensuring common areas are always presentable and well-maintained 
 Keep kitchens well stocked; manage coffee, kitchen, and office supply orders and vendor relationships 
 Liaise with the landlord on building maintenance, deficiencies, cleaning schedules, and temperature or lighting issues 
 Install small accessories (whiteboards, load bars, etc.) and move or arrange small furniture items as needed 
 Arrange for maintenance and repair of office equipment and furniture 
 Maintain accurate floor plans and up-to-date workstation information 
 Manage office supply inventory and purchase orders with vendors 
 
   
  Health &amp; Safety  
 
 Coordinate, attend, and take minutes for Joint Health &amp; Safety Committee meetings; action any requests or recommendations following each meeting 
 Perform duties of the Fire Warden during fire drills and emergencies 
 Conduct quarterly Health &amp; Safety inspections and follow up on identified issues 
 
   
  Co-op Recruitment Coordination  
 
 Own the end-to-end coordination of Solace’s co-op recruitment process in partnership with the HR team 
 Liaise with post-secondary institutions and co-op portals to post roles and manage incoming applications 
 Schedule interviews, coordinate assessments, and ensure a positive, organized candidate experience throughout the process 
 Onboard incoming co-op students, including workspace setup, introductions, and day-one logistics 
 Track co-op cycles, timelines, and headcount to keep the program running smoothly term over term 
 
   
  General Administration  
 
 Participate in team meetings, cross-departmental meetings, and working groups as required 
 Perform other related administrative duties as required, including managing purchase orders and expense tracking 
 
   
  What You Bring  
 
 Minimum 2-year college diploma in a related field (Business Administration, Office Management, etc.) and/or equivalent experience 
 3+ years of experience in an office management, facilities, or administrative role in a professional environment 
 A genuinely warm, people-first personality. You love making others feel welcome and go out of your way to do so 
 Strong organizational skills and sharp attention to detail; you take ownership of your responsibilities and see things through 
 Comfortable managing vendor relationships, coordinating with contractors, and researching solutions independently 
 Flexible, resourceful, and ready to GSD (get stuff done!) 
 Ability to thrive under pressure, manage competing priorities, and adapt quickly when plans change 
 Proficient in MS Outlook and the full MS Office Suite 
 Able to occasionally lift objects weighing up to 20 lbs, receive and deliver goods, and maintain the storage room 
 Experience with co-op or recruitment coordination is a strong asset 
 Health &amp; Safety Certification is an asset
