Renesas is seeking a Senior Analog IC Design Engineer for our Power Product Group, Performance Computing Power Team in Catania (Italy). Reporting to the Director of Engineering, you will have responsibility for the design, verification and validation of power management IC’s that supply the world’s largest and most advanced computing platforms. Your responsibilities encompass the full design cycle spanning from product definition to IC validation.  Responsibilities   Perform transistor-level design of mixed-signal power management circuit blocks  Evaluate tradeoffs among different circuit topologies for performance and area  Oversee the work of Layout Engineers  Write design review documentation, prepare verification and validation plans  Conduct design reviews and provide feedback to ensure design quality and compliance with specifications  Participate in test specification development and support Test Engineers  Support Validation Engineer during lab debug and datasheet compliance testing  Support Application Engineering and Product Engineering     BSc with 5+ years, MSc with 3+ years, or PhD in Electrical Engineering  Knowledge of CMOS and bipolar transistors, diodes, resistors, capacitors, inductors  Knowledge of basic circuit blocks (voltage/current references, current mirrors, amplifiers, comparators, oscillators and clocking circuits, digital gates)  Knowledge of feedback control theory and systems  Knowledge of low-voltage (1V-5V) and high-voltage (12V-100V) BCD technologies  Knowledge of design and analysis technique for analog circuit blocks (process/voltage/temperature corners and Montecarlo statistical simulations)  Familiarity with Cadence design tools  Familiarity with switch-mode and linear regulator topologies  Familiarity with electronic test equipment  Good communication skills  Good knowledge of written and spoken English   Preferred Skills and Experience   Knowledge of precision current sensing techniques  Knowledge of regulated or unregulated charge pump circuits  Knowledge of high precision rail-to-rail amplifiers  Knowledge of integrated-FET switching regulators and PMICs  Knowledge of behavioral modeling techniques  Knowledge of automated test methodologies    The expected annual pay range for this position is €39,000–€52,000.
