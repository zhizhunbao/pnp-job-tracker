## Overview

This page provides information on nominations issued and available, applications to be processed, estimated processing times, processing order and priorities and draws.

All information about nominations issued, nomination allocation distribution, spaces remaining and applications to be processed are subject to change without notice. AAIP reserves the right to redistribute allocations to other streams or pathways at any time.

In 2026, the Alberta Advantage Immigration Program (AAIP) will prioritize draws and nominations within its worker streams for occupations in key sectors such as health care, technology, construction, manufacturing, aviation, agriculture, and communities designated under the Rural Renewal Stream. While these sectors represent the program’s primary focus areas for the year, they are not an exhaustive list. This guidance is intended to help manage expectations and highlight AAIP’s highest priorities for 2026.

## Additional federal spaces

### Additional federal immigration spaces for physicians or Francophones

Up to 10,000 federal immigration spaces are available across all provincial nominee programs for provinces and territories to nominate practice‑ready physicians or Francophones (in Canada or abroad).

Any AAIP nomination issued in 2026 for a physician or Francophone who meets the federal criteria for this initiative will not count toward Alberta’s 6,403 nomination allocation.

### Eligibility

- Physicians: Must be NOC 31100, 31101, or 31102 and be practice‑ready (meeting AAIP’s Dedicated Health Care Pathway requirements).
- Francophones: May work in any AAIP‑eligible NOC and must meet CLB/NCLC 5 in French across all four competencies.

AAIP will publish the number of nominations issued under this initiative as they are confirmed with IRCC for both Express Entry and non‑Express Entry pathways.

No new streams or pathways are being created for this initiative; existing AAIP stream designs apply. For details on how practice‑ready physicians may typically qualify for AAIP, see AAIP’s Dedicated Health Care Pathway page.

## Draw information

- Information will be updated as draws are conducted. AAIP draws are not regularly scheduled. They are done as needed to address sector needs and provincial priorities.
- EOI score is not the only factor AAIP uses to select candidates for invitation. AAIP considers a variety of factors provided in Worker EOIs to meet program priorities. To protect program integrity AAIP does not disclose recent draw parameters. For general information on Alberta’s labour market, consult the Government of Alberta’s [Labour Market Information website](https://labour-market.alberta.ca/).
