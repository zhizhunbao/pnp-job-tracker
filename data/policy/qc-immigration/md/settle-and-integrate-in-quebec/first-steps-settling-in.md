# Premières démarches d’installation (First steps to settling in) Information Session

The Premières démarches d’installation information session takes you through the steps for settling in Québec upon your arrival.

## On this page:

## Description of the Information Session

### Location

The session is given by several partner organizations of the Gouvernement du Québec, throughout Québec.

### Session duration

The information session lasts three hours.

### Free of charge

Participation in the event is free.

### Language

The session is offered in French only.

### Contents of the session

The Premières démarches d’installation session covers the following topics:

- Important documents to obtain upon arriving in Québec (health insurance, social insurance, driver’s licence, etc.)
- Opening a bank account
- Looking for a place to live
- Daycare services and registering children for school
- The Québec education system
- Tax credits, financial assistance for families and income tax returns
- Your rights as consumers
- Settling in the regions
- Free settlement and integration services offered by [partnering community organizations](https://www.quebec.ca/en/immigration/partners)
- Employment services offered by public services and their partners
- The Accompagnement Québec [integration service for immigrants](https://www.quebec.ca/en/immigration/integration-service-for-immigrants)
- [Comparative evaluation for studies done outside Québec](https://www.quebec.ca/en/immigration/work-quebec/recognition-skills-acquired-abroad/getting-comparative-evaluation)
- The [Objectif Intégration session](https://www.quebec.ca/en/immigration/settle-and-integrate-in-quebec/objectif-integration)
- Democratic values and the Québec values expressed in the *Charter of Human Rights and Freedom*

## Conditions for admission

To participate in the Premières démarches d’installation (First steps to settling in) session, you must meet the following conditions.

### Age

You must be 18 years of age or older.

### Knowledge of French

You must have an intermediate knowledge of French to participate in the session.

### Status for Eligibility

You must meet one of the following conditions:

- You are a naturalized Canadian citizen.
- You are a permanent resident of Canada.
- You are a temporary resident and hold a permit issued for the purpose of eventual permanent residence or a permit from the Minister of Immigration, Francization and Integration.
- You are a person authorized to submit an application for permanent residence in Canada.
- You are in Québec as a temporary worker for one or more consecutive periods of one year or more.
- You are a foreign student in Québec for a period of more than six months.
- You are a protected person in Canada (who has been granted refugee protection).

### If you are working or studying temporarily in Québec

Make sure you meet the conditions of your status.

If you do not comply with these conditions, you may:

- Lose your status.
- Be refused subsequent entry into Québec or elsewhere in Canada.

For more information, contact [Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada](https://www.canada.ca/en/immigration-refugees-citizenship/corporate/contact-ircc.html).

## How to register

You can register for the Premières démarches d’installation with a partner organization of the Gouvernement du Québec that offers an information session.

Certain organizations offer a drop-in daycare during the session.

For more assistance, contact the [Centre de contacts clientèle (Information service) of the Ministère de l’lmmigration, de la Francisation et de l’Intégration](https://www.quebec.ca/en/gouvernement/ministeres-organismes/immigration/coordonnees-structure/contact-us).
