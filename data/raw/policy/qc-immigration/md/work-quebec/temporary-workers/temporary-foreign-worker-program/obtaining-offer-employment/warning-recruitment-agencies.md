# Warning if you use the services of a recruitment agency for temporary foreign workers

Some agencies recruit workers abroad for Québec employers. Make sure to obtain good information before finding work in Québec with the assistance of a recruitment agency.

Here are some precautions to take if you obtain a job through an agency.

- Check if the recruitment agency has [its recruitment agency licence](https://www.cnesst.gouv.qc.ca/fr/demarches-formulaires/agences-placement-personnel-recrutement/permis-agences/registre-titulaires-permis-agence);
- Check the origin of the offer of employment by consulting the website of the employer who is offering the job, for instance;
- Obtain information about your rights as a temporary foreign worker.

You do not have to pay recruitment fees. The only fees the employer may ask you to assume are:

- The fees required to review your application for temporary selection
- The work permit fees
- A portion of the air fares in the case of the [Seasonal Agricultural Worker Program (SAWP)](https://www.canada.ca/en/immigration-refugees-citizenship/services/work-canada/permit/agricultural-workers-work-temporarily.html). (The employer can ask the worker to cover airfare costs in part or in full under the Global Talent Stream, High-Salary Stream, and Simplified Processing. He cannot require it for the agricultural stream, the low-wage stream, and home care providers.)

Also, a recruitment agency cannot require you to give it custody of:

- Personal documents, such as your passport or your health insurance card; or
- Goods that belong to you.

If in doubt, do not hesitate to contact:

- [The Commission des normes, de l’équité, de la santé et de la sécurité au travail](https://www.cnesst.gouv.qc.ca/en/client-services/contact-us); or
- [The Customer Contact Centre of the ministère de l’Immigration, de la Francisation et de l’Intégration](https://www.quebec.ca/gouvernement/ministere/immigration/coordonnees).
